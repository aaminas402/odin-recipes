Idli Recipe

A south Indian Fluffy dish made from rice

Idli is a soft, fluffy, steamed rice cake that holds a cherished place in South Indian cuisine and is enjoyed across India as a nutritious and light breakfast dish. Made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram), idli is a perfect blend of taste and health. Its spongy texture and subtle flavor pair beautifully with tangy sambar, creamy coconut chutney, or a drizzle of ghee and podi (spiced lentil powder). Known for its easy digestibility and balanced nutrition, idli is a versatile meal, often seen as a comfort food. Simple yet wholesome, it’s a staple that reflects the culinary wisdom of traditional Indian kitchens.

Ingredients:

  • Idli rice: 2 cups
  • Urad dal (whole, skinned black gram): 1 cup
  • Fenugreek seeds (optional): ½ tsp
  • Salt to taste
  • Water to taste

Steps:

  1. Soak the Ingredients

    • Wash the idli rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 4–6 hours.
    • Rinse the urad dal and fenugreek seeds and soak them together for 4–6 hours in a separate bowl.

  2. Grind the Batter

    • Drain the soaked urad dal and fenugreek seeds. Grind them in a wet grinder or blender with small amounts of water.
    • Drain the rice and grind it into a slightly coarse batter, adding water as needed
    • Mix the urad dal and rice batters together in a large bowl.

  3. Ferment the Batter

    • Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment overnight (6–12 hours) in a warm place.

  4. Steam the Idlis

    • Grease idli plates lightly with oil or ghee. Fill each mold with the fermented batter
    • Steam the idlis in a steamer for 10–12 minutes until they are cooked through.
    • Remove the idlis from the molds and serve hot with chutney, sambar, or podi.
