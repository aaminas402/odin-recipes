Puttu Recipe

A south Indian Breakfast dish made from rice

Puttu is a traditional South Indian dish, especially popular in Kerala and Tamil Nadu, made by steaming layers of coarsely ground rice flour and grated coconut. This cylindrical delicacy is soft, crumbly, and mildly flavored, making it a versatile breakfast option. Often served with kadala curry (spiced black chickpeas), ripe bananas, or even drizzled with ghee and sugar, puttu offers a delightful balance of textures and flavors. Its preparation, using minimal oil and no frying, makes it a healthy choice. Whether enjoyed with savory curries or sweet accompaniments, puttu embodies the simplicity and richness of South Indian cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • Rice flour (roasted): 2 cups
  • Grated coconut: 1 cup
  • Salt: ½ tsp
  • Water: As needed (approximately ¾ cup)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Flour

    • Mix Flour and Salt
    • Add Water Gradually
    • Check Consistency

  2. Layer the Puttu Maker

    • Prepare the Steamer
    • Layer Coconut and Flour
    • Do Not Overfill

  3. Steam the puttu

    • Assemble the Puttu Maker
    • Steam Cook
    • Serve Hot
